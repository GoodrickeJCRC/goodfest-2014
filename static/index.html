Line up

Young Kato

Young Kato are an indie pop band from Cheltenham and Birmingham that formed in December 2011. The group was formed by vocalist Tommy Wright, guitarists Jack Edwards and Joe Green, bassist Joe Lever, keyboardist Harry Steele and drummer Sam Henderson. The band's debut self-titled EP was released 26 August 2012 and reached number 31 in the iTunes albums chart.

Glass Caves

You might have already seen Glass Caves whilst entering retail therapy through the city centre of York. This pontefract outfit have taken York to their heart busking on the streets every weekend and building a huge following, and even scribing a fan favourite "Eboracum" with the city's Roman moniker.

Busking as a 3 piece, but live and electric as a 5 piece Glass Caves fuse agitated rhythms, yo-yoing guitars and dissonant keyboard sounds with vocals that splinter then soar through their songs. They come at you like a band channelling the spirit of American alternative bands, such as Local Natives and Fleet Foxes, through a very British filter to produce perfectly formed morsels of ballsy alt-rock.

King's Parade

Described as "a pretty groovy retro jazz pop thing with soul touches", The King's Parade are a four piece Soul/Pop band with influences from Motown and Blues. Taking their energetic live shows to the streets of London, they have started to build a strong following. Their songs have received amazing feedback from fans all over the world, with tunes such as 'Vagabond' reaching over 130,000 plays on SoundCloud.

Hitting Goodricke on June 14th

GoodFest is the biggest free annual festival hosted by the best college at the University of York: Goodricke! Originally started in 2009 as a small one-day festival, this year is the festival's fifth anniversary and is set to be bigger and better than ever!

The entire event will be held around the Goodricke College nucleus on Heslington East, with a bar offering cheap drinks and a variety of food options. In the daytime, there will be many attractions to look forward to, such as a shisha lounge, comedians, poets, local music talent and high-energy games. The evening will see live music acts until 11pm, followed by the official after party at the Glasshouse, hosted by Insomnia Sounds.

Make sure Saturday 14th June is free in your diary, and keep an eye out for more official GoodFest announcements coming soon. Join the Facebook event for instant updates!
